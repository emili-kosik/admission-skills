# Draft critique rubric

Five dimensions, scored 1–5. Every score is justified by exactly one quoted
line from the draft. The memo ends with at most three revision questions —
questions, never rewrites — and a mechanical-flags list (error type + location,
no corrected text shown).

| Dimension | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Voice** | Could have been written by any applicant or a committee; thesaurus words the student would never say aloud | Recognizably a teenager, but hedged and formal in patches | Sounds like one specific person; you could pick this student out of a hallway; risk-taking sentences only they would write |
| **Specificity (show-don't-tell)** | Abstractions and adjectives ("passionate", "impactful"); zero proper nouns, zero sensory detail | Some concrete scenes but summarized; details generic enough to transplant into another student's essay | Named people, places, objects, numbers; at least one detail no other applicant on Earth could supply |
| **Insight / reflection** | Narrates events; the "lesson" is a cliché stated in the last paragraph | Genuine reflection but stops at the first obvious meaning | Thinks on the page; complicates its own first answer; the realization reframes the opening |
| **Structure & momentum** | Chronological résumé walk; the reader can stop anywhere | Clear arc but slow start or rushed ending; paragraph 1 is throat-clearing | The first sentence earns the second; every paragraph changes something; ends somewhere the start couldn't see |
| **Prompt responsiveness** | Prompt ignored or answered in one bolted-on sentence | Answers the prompt but generically; (PIQ) drifts past 350 words or buries the answer | The prompt's question is the essay's spine; (PIQ) answers in the first two sentences and spends all 350 words on evidence |

## Memo format

```markdown
# Critique — <draft file>, round <N> (<date>)

Policies cited this project: <schools + tiers>   ← E8 log line

## Scores
- Voice: 3/5 — "<quoted line>" ...one sentence of why.
- Specificity: ...
- Insight: ...
- Structure: ...
- Prompt responsiveness: ...

## Revision questions (max 3)
1. <question about the student's own material>
2. ...

## Mechanical flags
- <error type>, <location> (e.g., subject-verb agreement, paragraph 2, sentence 3)
```

## Calibration notes

- A first draft scoring 2s and 3s is *normal and good* — say so.
- Never average the scores into a verdict; the dimensions are diagnostics.
- If two rounds pass with no movement on a dimension, change the question,
  not the intensity ("What did the room smell like?" beats "add more detail").
- The reader's attention is the scarce resource: note the exact line where a
  cold reader would look up (the AO-lens observation from `essay-reviewer`).
