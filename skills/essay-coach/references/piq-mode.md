# UC Personal Insight Question mode

The UC application replaces the personal statement with 8 Personal Insight
Questions; the student answers **any 4, 350 words each** (verbatim prompts in
`data/essay_prompts.json → uc_piq`). UC states it runs plagiarism and AI
checks on PIQs — unattributed AI content risks the application across all
nine campuses. Surface this at PIQ-mode start.

## These are answers, not essays

The craft is fundamentally different from the 650-word narrative:

- **First-sentence responsiveness.** The reader is scoring against the
  question. The best PIQs answer it in the opening line, then spend the rest
  on evidence. There is no word budget for scene-setting.
- **No recommendations corroborate claims** for most UC applicants — concrete
  specifics carry all the evidentiary weight. "I led the robotics build team
  of 11" beats any adjective.
- **350 words is a discipline, not a limit to approach.** Every sentence must
  either state the answer or evidence it.

## Portfolio thinking across the four

The four chosen PIQs are read together as one picture. Coach the *selection*
before any drafting:

1. List which of the 8 questions the student has real material for.
2. Choose 4 that cover **different facets** — leadership ≠ creativity ≠
   challenge ≠ academic passion. Two answers about the same activity from
   different angles is usually one answer too many.
3. Check the portfolio against the rest of the application: what does the
   activities list already show? PIQs should add, not repeat.

## PIQ-specific critique adjustments

- Prompt-responsiveness dimension is scored hardest: answered in the first
  two sentences?
- Voice matters, but directness outranks lyricism here.
- Flag any answer spending >100 words before its first concrete fact.
- Word counts: the snapshot hook reports against the 350 limit when the essay
  is registered in `essays/index.json` with `word_limit: 350`.

## Calendar note

UC filing closes Nov 30 — a full month before most RD deadlines. For a
UC-heavy list, PIQ drafting belongs in September–October, not December.
The `roadmap` skill owns the dates; this file owns the craft.
