# Supplemental essay archetypes

Which supplements each college requires comes from `data/deadlines.json`
(writing/portfolio flags) and the college's application — verify there.
This file is the craft guidance per archetype.

| Archetype | The real question being asked | Coach toward | Kill on sight |
|---|---|---|---|
| **"Why us"** | Have you done your homework, and will you enroll and thrive here? | 2–3 *specific* resources (a named course, a professor's lab, a program, a tradition) each tied to the student's demonstrated history. The test: the essay should break if you swap in another college's name. Visit notes in `colleges.json` are the raw material. | Rankings talk, campus-is-beautiful, brochure paraphrase, "prestigious" |
| **Community** | What do you carry from a group, and what do you give back? | Define community narrowly (the 6 a.m. swim lane, the ESL church basement, the Tuesday chess table) and show a *role*, not a membership | Three sentences defining the word "community"; résumé-in-paragraph-form |
| **Challenge / adversity** | How do you respond when things break? | 20% event, 80% response and change; agency verbs; what the student *did* | Trauma-dumping with no reflection; borrowed hardship; competing in the suffering olympics |
| **Extracurricular elaboration** | Go deeper on one activity | One micro-story *inside* the activity — a specific failure, decision, or moment — not the activity's history | Repeating the 150-character activities-list description with more adjectives |
| **Intellectual curiosity** | Do you actually like learning? | A specific rabbit hole and where it led: the question, the sources, the dead ends, the next question | "I've always been passionate about…"; a book report |
| **Short answers (≤50 words)** | Quick texture check | Answer literally, with personality; one concrete detail beats a compressed philosophy | Recycled essay fragments; quotation-of-a-famous-person openers |

## Reuse discipline

Supplement material can be adapted across colleges, but "why us" essays
cannot — each must pass the swap test. Track which draft serves which college
in `essays/index.json` (`target` = the college name) so nothing is submitted
with the wrong school's name in it (it happens every year; check for it
explicitly in the final read).

## All ten rules apply

Archetype guidance changes what questions you ask the student — never who
writes. E1–E10 in the main skill hold for every supplement, including
50-word short answers.
